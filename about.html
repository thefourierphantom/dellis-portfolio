Background

I came into cybersecurity from Electrical Engineering — not as a pivot, but as an expansion. The physical world and the digital world are converging, and most practitioners can only see one side of that boundary. The EE background gives me visibility at the layer most people don't reach: signals, hardware, firmware, physical threat vectors.

My core lane is DFIR and threat intelligence, with a deliberate push into signals security and the hardware/cyber intersection. I've published CASE-001 — a network forensics investigation reconstructing host persistence from 7,000+ frames under NIST SP 800-61 — and I'm building Trace, a solo threat intelligence fusion workbench, for HackUSF 2026.

Outside of technical work, I'm a research assistant at the USF CIBR Lab under Dr. George Burruss, contributing to AI-driven cybercrime victim support and a new OSINT study on student digital footprint exposure. I also serve as Technical Development Chair for NSBE USF and as a Future Security Practitioners volunteer with the USF Global and National Security Institute.

The long-term target is founding the Tactical Intelligence Group — a defense technology firm operating at the intersection of DFIR, signals intelligence, and unmanned systems. Every piece of work right now is building toward that.
